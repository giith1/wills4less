So What is a Property Protection Trust?

What is a Property Protection Trust? A Property Protection Trust, such as a Home Property Protection Trust Hampshire, is a document in which you (the settlor) formally put your house, building land or buy-to-let property into a trust which you have set up. You select a trustee (a trustworthy person or professional), who has power over the property in accordance with your instructions. When you pass away or if you become incapacitated, a successor trustee takes care of implementing your plans, simplifying the transfer of assets to your heirs and, potentially, avoiding probate.

A few benefits of a Property Protection Trust are:

Protecting assets from between-family feuds and creditors

Preventing probate delays and expenses

Disclosure of privacytrust details is not public information

Transparent, predictable management of assets for the people that matter to you

How does a Property Protection Trust help save my loved ones time and money?

Let’s suppose a typical scenario: A couple own their home together. And one dies and bequests everything to the other. (Any surviving spouse would later remarry. If they subsequently die, the home may go to the new spouse or possibly their children-and not the children from the first marriage at all.

That happens more frequently than one might imagine.

By establishing a Property Protections Trust, you protect your interest in the property from being diverted intentionally or otherwise and ensure that it goes ultimately where you want it to go, in the hands of your intended beneficiaries.

How really does the Property Safety Trust Hampshire function?

----------------------------

If the Property Protection Trust is set out as part of your Will, on your death, your half of the home will go into the trust. Your surviving partner may be allowed to live in the property for life but your share will not go to them, it will instead go to the people you choose (referred to as a ‘life interest’).

Here’s a simple breakdown:

You are joint tenants to your home with your partner.

You place your share into a Property Protections Trust within your Will

Your partner lives in the same property without the risk of losing it

When they die, your share passes on immediately to your children or recipients.

This arrangement provides a long-term future for your family without evicting you( r partner) from your home.

Can You Still Sell Your Home if You Have A Property Protection Trust?

Yes. With a Property Protections Trust you’re not tied to one house. If you ever relocate or downsize, the trust’s share slides over to your new home so you get to keep your share (i.e., your assumed value) safe.

Has a Property Protection Trust an Impact on Inheritance Tax?

A Property Protection Trust itself can’t mitigate Inheritance Tax, but it can make sure that your share of the property goes to the right people. Once it exists, it can be accompanied by other trusts and strategies for tax planning. With Will 4 Less, we'll lead you straight on the optimal choices.
