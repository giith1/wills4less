WHAT TO CONSIDER, WHEN LOOKING AT A WILL WRITING SERVICES

Trust and Trustees

Many people fail to recognize the importance of selecting trustees when preparing their will. Trustees are individuals entrusted with managing your assets on behalf of your family, including minor children under 18.

Guardian

If you have young children, it's crucial to choose a person to care for them until they reach adulthood, typically 18 in most states. You have the right to designate a guardian for your children in your will, ensuring their well-being is entrusted to someone you trust.

Beneficiaries

A will ensures that your loved ones inherit the possessions you have worked hard to acquire. It is important to thoughtfully decide how your property will be distributed and who will inherit what. Beneficiaries can include your children, spouse or partners, close relatives (such as aunts, uncles, and grandchildren), friends, co-workers, or anyone else you choose.

Single Will

A single will is a legally binding document that outlines an individual’s wishes regarding the distribution of their assets and property after death. It designates who will inherit their belongings and specifies how these assets should be allocated.

Mirror Will

A mirror will consists of two wills, typically created by spouses or partners, which contain nearly identical provisions. These wills reflect each person’s wishes, ensuring that their estate is passed on to their chosen beneficiaries.

Urgent Will Supplement

Life can change quickly! Keep your will updated to reflect significant events. Whether it’s the birth of a child or a new inheritance, make sure your assets are distributed according to your wishes. Call us today!

Discretionary Trust

A discretionary trust is a type of trust where the trustee has the discretion to decide how the trust’s income or assets will be distributed among the beneficiaries.

Flexible Life Interest Trust

Take control of your estate planning with a Flexible Life Interest Trust (FLIT). This arrangement safeguards your assets while ensuring benefits for future generations.

Property Protection

A property protection trust is a legal arrangement where ownership of a person’s assets is transferred to a trust, benefiting both the individual and other designated beneficiaries.

Official FAQs
Do I need to use will writing services?

Yes, it is essential! Having a written Will is crucial. For example, if you live in England and pass away without a formal document outlining your wishes, the government will decide how your assets are distributed. This may result in funds being used for public projects rather than being passed on to your loved ones. Taking the time to create a Will ensures that your wishes are respected and your estate is managed according to your preferences.

What information will I need to write a Will?

To create a will, you will need to provide several key details, such as your full name and address, the names of your chosen executors, a detailed list and description of all your assets, and information regarding any minor children you have.

How can I provide for adults with special needs in my Will?

To ensure that adults with special needs receive their inheritance in a way that benefits them, it’s advisable to set up a Trust. This allows for regular disbursements rather than a lump-sum payment, providing long-term financial support.

I have a will, but I am getting married soon. Is my will still valid?

Your will remains valid until you get married. However, marriage automatically revokes any previous will, which means it will no longer be valid. It's important to create a new will after your marriage to ensure your assets are distributed according to your wishes, as your possessions may otherwise be subject to intestacy laws.

Do I need a solicitor to make a Will?

No, you are not required to have a solicitor to create a Will. You can write it yourself, or choose to seek help from a professional Will writer if you prefer.

Why do I need two witnesses when writing a Will?

Under UK law, for a Will to be legally valid and enforceable in court, it must be signed in the presence of two independent adult witnesses who are over 18 years old.
