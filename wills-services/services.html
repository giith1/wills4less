A will certainly and testament is a lawful record that shares an individual's (testator) desires regarding how their property (estate) is to be distributed after their fatality and regarding which person (executor) is to handle the residential or commercial property till its last circulation. For the circulation (devolution) of property not figured out by a will, see inheritance and intestacy. Though it has been believed a "will" historically used only to real estate, while "testament" applied only to personal property (therefore triggering the prominent title of the paper as "last will and testimony"), records show the terms have actually been utilized interchangeably. Therefore, the word "will" validly relates to both individual and real property. A will certainly might also produce a testamentary trust fund that is effective just after the death of the testator.

What Is a Single Will?

A single will is a legal document that specifies how an individual’s assets, finances, and personal belongings should be distributed after their passing. The term "single" distinguishes it from a joint will, which is typically created by married couples to address their combined estates.

Our goal is to provide a seamless, efficient service that ensures the proper distribution of your assets according to your wishes. With personalized support, your dedicated advisor can even represent you in court if necessary.

A single will is tailored to fit your unique circumstances and preferences. To help you get started, we offer a complimentary consultation with our experienced team of will writers. DO YOU NEED A MIRROR WILL ?

A Will is a vital legal document that allows you to clearly outline your wishes regarding the distribution of your assets—such as property, money, and possessions—as well as decisions about your children’s care, in the event of your death.

If you live in the UK and pass away without a valid Will, including a Mirror Will, the government takes control of how your assets are distributed. This could leave others with the ability to make decisions about your underage children’s care and education that may not align with your wishes.
